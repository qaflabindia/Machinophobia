AI Capability-Adoption Gap Analysis

Technical Capability vs. Real-World Implementation Across 15 Occupational Categories

AI Capability Score (0-100) - Technical capability to perform core tasks
Adoption Velocity Score (0-100) - Actual implementation rate

🔍 Key Insights: The Capability-Adoption Chasm

  • Largest Gaps (Highest Transformation Risk):
    • Office & Administrative: 90 capability / 35 adoption = 55-point gap
    • Healthcare (Administrative): 75 capability / 25 adoption = 50-point gap
    • Legal Occupations: 85 capability / 38 adoption = 47-point gap
  • Fastest Adopters: Computer & Mathematical (75% adoption), Sales (65%), Customer Service (62%)
  • The Pattern: White-collar, knowledge work shows highest capability but variable adoption rates
  • Lower Risk (For Now): Physical Labor (25/15), Personal Care (30/20) - but robotics advancing
  • Critical Finding: Every occupation shows a capability-adoption gap, indicating widespread transformation is technically feasible but not yet deployed

Data Sources: Anthropic Research (2026), Yale Budget Lab, U.S. Census Bureau ABS (2023-2025), BLS Projections (2024-2034)

Capability scores based on technical feasibility assessments; Adoption scores incorporate current usage data, barrier analysis, and deployment velocity
